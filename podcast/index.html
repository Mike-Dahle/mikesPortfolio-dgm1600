Loremasters

The process behind the podcast

Goals

Welcome to my podcast called Loremasters. This is a podcast that I did as an assignment for my Digital Media 2260 class at Utah Valley University. The goal of this podcast was to create a professional sounding and looking podcast from the ground up; Utilizing the information we learned about podcasting in this course. We each chose a topic we are interested in and then created two episodes of a podcast pertaining to that topic.

Production Process

Because I don't have access to a professional setup with a soundproof room and professional grade microphones I had to improvise a little bit. But the cool thing is, I think that the podcast sounds pretty good considering what I used. Each episode was recorded in one take on my 2015 Macbook Pro. I used the DAW software Ableton to record the audio. But the equipment I used was just my laptops microphone. I did have the benefit of a moderately sound insulated room as well, complements of my schools music facilities.

Testing & Editing

Each episode before I would start the actual recording I would take test recordings to make sure that everything was working and sounded correct. Once I was satisfied with how things sounded I would then record the entire episode in one take. I actually didn't feel like I needed cut out or change any of the audio afterwards, which was convenient. That's not to say that the audio you hear on the podcast is the raw input. I went into Ableton after recording and made sure to EQ the vocals, add a Drum Buss, and then I also used a filter to warm up the low frequencies. (the macbook above shows an actual screenshot of my recording if you are curious what it looks like.)

Publishing

This podcast has been published on Soundcloud. I chose to publish it initially on Soundcloud because I have previous experience using their website. While more popular apps and websites exist like Spotify or Apple Music I though it would be a good idea to stick to what I know at the moment.
